DNA Analysis Project

Project Overview:

In this project we used different techinques to create billions of copies of our DNA, allowing us to find people in our class that matches strands of our DNA. We manipulated the cells inside our cheek to achieve this, and documented the entire project wiht out teams, eventually making a final documentary To start off we created a stop motion explaining .

Procedure:

First, DNA extraction is performed by scraping the inside of our mouth with a toothpick, extracting the cells on our cheeks. We then put them into an extraction buffer and heated them for 10 minutes, allowing them to burst, and releasing the genetic material. Next we performed PCR Amplification, which replicated the targeted cells by adding different ingredients, such as Polymerase, Primers, and Nucleotide Bases. We cycled through different temperatures 30 times, heating it up, splitting the DNA and cooling it down creating billions of copies. The third step was Gel Electrophoresis. This is used to separate the copies made by size. A gel is poured into a mold with a comb that forms little pockets for our samples. These are then put into an electric current, which sorts the DNA by pulling the smaller fragments farther through the gel compared to the longer fragments. We then illuminate the gel to see our results. Finally we do our Data Analysis, which is conducted by capturing images of the glowing gel, using our editing software, Adobe Photoshop, to analyze the bands, and input our findings into our spreadsheet, which performs calculations for us.

21'st Century Skills

One 21st century skill I feel I grew during this project was my ability to Access and Analyze information. The main goal of this project was to analyze our results and find our matches, meaning we present time discussing what we actually see on our photos, and what it means. Although I did not have results myself, me and my team came to multiple conclusions throughout this project. We noticed that, despite our predictions, the majority of matches came from people who were not clearly ethnically or historically related. We found this out by analyzing our spreadsheets, and having clear cut conversations on what each of them meant. This overall helped me to grow my skills by talking it out and discussing how and why we got the results we did.
